About

Hi, I'm Linda. I'm a bookkeeper by profession, which means I spend a lot of time making numbers behave and occasionally wondering why receipts seem to reproduce when no one is looking.

I run my own bookkeeping business where I help small businesses stay organized, keep their finances straight, and avoid the mysterious panic that happens when someone opens their accounting software for the first time in six months.

When I'm not wrangling spreadsheets, I'm learning about digital marketing and graphic design, mostly because I like making aesthetic artwork that converts, and figuring out how to make websites work, and how ideas can be shared online. This site is a place where I experiment, build things, and occasionally prove that even bookkeepers can be creative.

Thanks for stopping by. If you brought receipts, we'll get along great.

Why I Like Web Development

Web development appeals to the same part of my brain that enjoys bookkeeping: the part that gets suspicious when something is even slightly out of place. A missing semicolon in code feels a lot like a missing dollar in an account reconciliation. Both require patience, careful attention, and a willingness to stare at tiny details until they confess.

I also enjoy the detective work. Patterns start to appear once you spend enough time with numbers or code. And when those patterns suddenly break, that's usually where the real problem is hiding.

What keeps me learning is a simple hope: that one day the website I imagine will show up on the screen exactly the way I pictured it. Until then, I'll keep adjusting code, fixing mistakes, and celebrating small victories when the page finally behaves the way it's supposed to.

My Real Boss: Max

No “About Me” page would be complete without mentioning Max, my Maltese poodle and the true manager of my daily schedule.

I originally got Max so he could take me for walks. Yes, you read that correctly. Technically I hold the leash, but everyone involved knows who’s really in charge. Without him, I’d probably spend far too many hours glued to a computer screen chasing one more line of code or reconciling one more account.

Max has a very reliable internal clock. When it’s time for a walk or dinner, he parks himself directly in front of me and stares with intense concentration, like a tiny, fluffy project manager reviewing my productivity. If I ignore him, he puts his snout on my knees and looks at me with the most adorable puppy eyes. But if that doesn't work, he escalates the situation by making small but very clear noises that translate roughly to, “Excuse me… it is break time.”

Because of him, I step outside, notice the weather, and remember there is a whole world beyond spreadsheets and web pages. Max makes sure I get fresh air, a little exercise, and a reminder that life should stay balanced.

Plus, every good web developer needs quality supervision. Max takes that responsibility very seriously.
